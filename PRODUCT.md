# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Competitive recreational runners and endurance athletes. Age 25-65. Checking their standing once or twice daily, on mobile (in the car after a morning run) and occasionally desktop (deep dive session). Their mode is performance orientation: they want to know exactly where they stand, how far behind the Buzzard they are, and whether they're on pace to finish. They don't need to be sold anything — they're already in the race.

Secondary users: families, coaches, curious spectators checking on participants.

## Product Purpose

GVRAT 2026 is the live tracking dashboard for the Great Virtual Race Across The States — a 679-mile virtual running race from Baxter Springs, KS to Pueblo, CO, running May through September 2026. The dashboard shows real-time leaderboard rankings, individual runner profiles with progress and activity history, and a Leaflet map of the route. Success means a runner can open the page, find themselves in under 5 seconds, understand their pace situation vs the Buzzard cutoff, and feel motivated to log more miles.

## Brand Personality

Athletic, precise, earned. Not corporate, not SaaS, not generic. The aesthetic reference is endurance sport data tools: UTMB Live, Strava athlete profiles, Suunto race reports. Understated confidence. Numbers big and proud. Geography matters — this race crosses America and the design should carry that weight.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (white cards, Inter font, teal accent, hero metric layout)
- Corporate HR/analytics tools (too clinical, feels like a pivot table)
- Fantasy sports apps (too gamified, cheap)
- RunSignUp's own UI (functional but 2012 aesthetic)
- Fitness tracker landing pages with giant gradient CTAs

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers carry the page.** If a stat matters to a runner, it should be large and legible at a glance. Small numbers in bordered boxes are an apology.
2. **Dark means serious.** The runner's identity section earns a dark, dramatic treatment — it's their race, their miles, their position. Light sections show the data.
3. **Color means something specific.** Gold = achievement and distance logged. Green = safe, ahead of Buzzard. Red = danger, behind Buzzard. Never used decoratively.
4. **Geography is the soul of this race.** The route from Kansas to Colorado is the emotional center. Topographic texture, place names, and route context belong in the design.
5. **No decoration that doesn't earn its space.** If an element doesn't communicate, cut it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Color is never the sole differentiator (always paired with text label or icon). Touch targets 44px minimum on mobile. Support for prefers-reduced-motion.
